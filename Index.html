Is consciousness the source of reality, or a product of it?

This is perhaps the oldest question science and spirituality have circled together. From a materialist view, consciousness emerges from matter — neurons firing, complexity arising. But quantum physics quietly whispers otherwise: the observer seems to participate in what is observed. Ancient Vedic thought held that Consciousness (with a capital C) is the very ground of being — not a byproduct, but the source. What if both are pointing at the same thing from opposite ends of the same tunnel?

What is a Spiritual Scientist?

Someone who refuses to choose between wonder and rigor. Who meditates in the morning and reads physics at night. Who holds a question the way a scientist holds a hypothesis — with discipline, curiosity, and the willingness to be completely wrong. This blog is that practice, made public.
